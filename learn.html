Simplifying Algebraic Expressions

2. Simplifying Algebraic Expressions

To simplify an algebraic expression, combine like terms by adding or subtracting their coefficients.

  • Example: 3x + 5x + 8x (Add the coefficients 3 and 5 to get 8)
  • Example: 4a - 2a + 7 = 2a + 7 (Subtract the coefficients 4 and 2 to simplify)
  • tip - Identify terms with the same variable and combine their coefficients.

3. Linear Equations

Linear equations have the form ax + b = c. To solve, isolate x by performing inverse operations.

4. Substitution

Replace variables with their given values to simplify expressions.

5. Factoring Algebraic Expressions

Factoring involves expressing an equation as a product of factors.

6. Expanding Brackets

Multiply every term inside the bracket by the factor outside.

7. Problem Solving with Algebra

Use algebra to model real-world problems and find solutions.
