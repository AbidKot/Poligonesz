Form 1 Algebra Notes

1. Algebraic Expressions

Algebraic expressions are mathematical phrases that can include numbers, variables, and arithmetic operations. They are used to represent real-world situations and perform calculations efficiently.

Examples:

  • 3x + 5
  • 4a - 7b
  • 2m² + 3m - 5

Simplifying Algebraic Expressions

To simplify, combine like terms by adding or subtracting their coefficients.

Example: 3x + 5x = 8x

2. Algebraic Operations

Addition and Subtraction: Combine like terms.

Multiplication: Multiply coefficients and variables separately.

Division: Divide coefficients and reduce common variables.

3. Linear Equations

Linear equations have the form ax + b = c. To solve, isolate x by performing inverse operations.

4. Substitution

Replace variables with their given values to simplify expressions.

5. Factoring Algebraic Expressions

Factoring involves expressing an equation as a product of factors.

6. Expanding Brackets

Multiply every term inside the bracket by the factor outside.

7. Problem Solving with Algebra

Use algebra to model real-world problems and find solutions.
